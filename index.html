01

Saving a 40%-of-ARR account from churn — in my first 15 days

Global enterprise • 15,000+ employees • Internal communication platform • APAC & US

The situation: Fifteen days into my role as founding CSM, I inherited an enterprise client representing roughly 40% of the regional ARR. The customer had just signed their contract believing the platform included advanced newsletter and audience targeting capabilities. These features did not yet exist. The client refused to proceed with onboarding.

What I did: I focused on three tracks simultaneously. First, I reviewed the contract with Sales to understand where expectations had diverged from product reality — then went back to the customer with transparency, not defensiveness. Second, I proposed a community-first, phased rollout that would deliver value immediately while we addressed the product gap. We co-designed a 90-day launch plan starting with 1,000 users, anchored by a CEO townhall video and gamification aligned to the company's culture of visibility and recognition. Third, I partnered with Product to design two new capabilities from user stories to delivery in 45 days — features that benefited the broader client base, not just this account.

I also set up biweekly regional check-ins and created local Ambassador users in every region — the first of its kind — who ran workshops and masterclasses to drive peer-led adoption.

02

Turning low adoption into a new revenue stream

AI platform company • 2,000 factory workers • Inventory optimization • India

The situation: An enterprise client had rolled out an AI-driven inventory optimization product to roughly 2,000 factory workers across sites in India. Despite multiple platform walkthroughs and training sessions, adoption remained at 23%. The client wasn't realizing operational value.

What I did: Instead of repeating the same training approach, I took a step back to understand the audience. Most end users were frontline workers who consumed information through short videos and WhatsApp — not formal dashboards or presentations. The gap wasn't product functionality; it was how we were communicating value in the wrong format for the audience.

I designed a series of multilingual DIY "How-to" videos recorded directly on the client's branded instance, plus a communication kit (posters, banners, WhatsApp assets) they could reuse in workshops and townhalls. This wasn't just a fix for one client — it became a scalable model.

03

Finding a use case the customer didn't know they needed

Global FMCG brand • 14 countries • Employee engagement platform • APAC & EMEA

The situation: A major FMCG brand had strong adoption of their employee communication platform but low engagement around corporate social responsibility — an area employees cared deeply about but had no structured way to participate in through the platform.

What I did: I identified the whitespace between what the platform offered and what employees actually wanted to do. I defined a new use case — a structured CSR engagement feature — and partnered with Product to design and build "Support My Cause" within the platform. I then rolled it out across 14 countries with governance, regional customization, and measurable participation tracking.

The customer didn't ask for this. I saw the gap between employee intent and platform capability, and turned it into a feature that deepened engagement and expanded the platform's value proposition.

How I'd deploy enterprise AI across 350,000 employees in India

A case study in scaling Claude adoption at a global IT services company — from signed contract to measurable business outcomes. This is how I think about the problem.

Goal: modernize legacy systems, accelerate software delivery, and support AI adoption among enterprise clients.

Stakeholder Map

Enterprise AI deployment at this scale requires alignment across six stakeholder groups. Each has different success criteria and engagement cadence.

Executive Sponsor

CTO / Chief AI Officer

Owns strategic AI vision. Cares about competitive differentiation and board-level narrative. Engaged through quarterly executive reviews and roadmap briefings.

Economic Buyer

VP Engineering / Head of Delivery

Controls budget across BUs. Needs ROI evidence per use case to justify investment. Engaged monthly with usage-to-outcome mapping.

Technical Champion

Platform Engineering / AI CoE Lead

Drives internal adoption, builds integrations, defines usage standards. Most critical for day-to-day success. Weekly technical syncs and product roadmap previews.

Adoption Owners

Business Unit Heads / Delivery Leads

Own P&L for specific practices. Need productivity gains in their teams. Engaged through BU-specific dashboards and use case workshops.

Enablement Partners

L&D / Internal Comms / HR

Design internal training and change management. Engaged through Train-the-Trainer co-design and rollout planning.

Governance

CISO / Data Privacy / Legal

Gate access and define acceptable use policies. Blockers if not engaged early. Need clarity on data handling and audit trails. Engaged pre-kickoff.

Center of Excellence

COE plan for the Software Development vertical

The goal isn't to create a permanent CS dependency. It's to build the customer's internal muscle so they can scale AI adoption independently. Here's how I'd build a COE for the software development practice — the vertical where Claude Code and API have the most immediate impact.

WEEKS 1–4: FOUNDATION
Charter & champions
  • Define COE mission and scope with CTO: "accelerate software delivery through AI-assisted development"
  • Identify 8–12 engineering leads as AI champions across delivery practices
  • Establish acceptable use policies with CISO — code review, IP, data handling
  • Build initial use case catalog: code generation, test writing, legacy modernization, PR review
  • Design Train-the-Trainer curriculum: prompt engineering for developers, Claude Code workflows, API integration patterns
  • Set up internal knowledge base for best practices and prompt libraries
WEEKS 5–10: ACTIVATE
Pilot & enable
  • Deliver first Train-the-Trainer cohort — AI champions become regional dev trainers
  • Launch Claude Code pilots in 3–4 priority engineering teams with measurable KPIs
  • Deploy API integrations in first production workloads (CI/CD, code review, documentation)
  • Build team-specific prompt libraries for common workflows
  • Run "office hours" for developer questions and pair programming with Claude
  • First usage data review: adoption rate, acceptance rate, productivity impact
WEEKS 11–16: SCALE
Transfer & expand
  • Expand beyond pilot teams using repeatable playbooks from Phase 2
  • Graduate AI champions to regional COE leads (India, US, Europe)
  • Publish internal case studies: "Team X shipped 2x faster with Claude Code"
  • Transition governance from CSM to internal COE team
  • Build innovation pipeline: new use cases sourced from within engineering org
  • Present first executive review with value realized vs. investment

Enterprise AI deployment isn't a product problem. It's an organizational change problem. The technology is ready. The question is whether the customer's organization is ready to absorb it — and the founding CSM's job is to make sure the answer is yes.
